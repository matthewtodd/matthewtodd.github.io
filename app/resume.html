Masifio Estates — Vegetable Ordering Application

Live at masifio.co.tz since August 2010, this application receives about 50 orders each week of produce to be shipped from a farm in southern Tanzania to up-market hotels and safari lodges. It receives customer and product lists from Quickbooks and generates packing lists and invoices in return. As internet access is frustratingly slow, I've paid close attention to load times, keeping pages light and assets aggressively cached. Rails, hosted on Heroku.

Health Care Technical Services — Dial-up Mail Server

To generate supplemental income, HCTS provide dial-up email access. I replaced their aging server with a Chef-configured Ubuntu system, providing a web interface for adding and removing customers.

Amani Children's Home — Holiday Card Fundraiser

Seasonally live at cards.amanikids.org each December, this site receives orders for greeting and gift cards, tracking inventory for multiple distributors and accepting payments by PayPal and JustGiving. Over the past 2 seasons, this app has handled hundreds of orders, representing over $20,000 raised for the Amani children. Rails, hosted on Heroku.

Amani Children's Home — Children's Database

This application helps Amani's social workers manage their caseloads and schedule their visits to children's families in the hopes of reunification. From their workflows, it also displays overview statistics for management staff. Rails, originally hosted internally, recently moved to Heroku and S3.

Amani Children's Home — Website

Live at amanikids.org since November 2008, replacing a 5-year-old PHP site. Rails, originally hosted on a Joyent Accelerator, since moved to Heroku and S3.

Amani Children's Home — Internal Server

This Chef-configured Ubuntu system hosted samba shares and the children's database up until its replacement in late 2010 by Dropbox and Heroku. It also provided a number of iptables restrictions in an attempt to reduce Amani's satellite internet charges.

OSX Application — HeadsUp

HeadsUp is a simple clone of GeekTool I wrote to explore OS X development — and because I found GeekTool's configuration far too distracting. The first few versions were in RubyCocoa; I've since rewritten it in Objective-C. I love HeadsUp for keeping my agenda on my desktop, but I can't seem to convince anyone else that it's awesome!

Ruby Gem — Captain

Ruby Gem — Shoe

Shoe DRYs up your Rake task configuration without the conceptual weight of Hoe and Jeweler.

Ruby Gem — Perquackey

Perquackey displays all the words that can be spelled with a given set of letters. It uses a C extension to avoid creating thousands of throwaway strings as it loops through the dictionary. Try it out at perquackey.matthewtodd.org.
